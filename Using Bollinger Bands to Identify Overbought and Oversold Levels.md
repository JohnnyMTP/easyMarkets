# Using Bollinger Bands® to Identify Overbought and Oversold Levels

A common phrase in trading is "the trend is your friend." However, even strong trends can stretch too far, increasing risk. **Bollinger Bands® indicators** are among the most trusted tools for determining when a market may be **overbought or oversold.**

Developed by John Bollinger, this [technical analysis](https://www.easy-markets.com/au/learn-centre/understanding-analysis/how-do-i-use-technical-analysis-to-trade/) indicator is applied across various markets, including stocks and forex. Traders use it to evaluate volatility and to anticipate possible turning points in price movement.

## Understanding Bollinger Bands®

Bollinger Bands® are **volatility-based bands** plotted above and below a moving average. They adjust automatically to changes in market conditions, widening during periods of higher volatility and narrowing when markets are quieter.

Traders use them to help determine:

-   Whether an asset is trading at an **unusually high or low level**
-   Possible **trend reversals or trend continuation** setups
-   Situations where the market may be **overbought or oversold**

## How Bollinger Bands® function

Bollinger Bands® are made up of three main elements:

• **Middle band:** A 20-period **Simple Moving Average (SMA)**
• **Upper band:** Two standard deviations **above** the SMA
• **Lower band:** Two standard deviations **below** the SMA

The distance between the bands expands and contracts in response to market volatility. This allows the indicator to adapt across different assets and timeframes.

Traders also apply Bollinger Bands® to:

• Identify **M-Tops**, which may signal bearish conditions, and **W-Bottoms**, which may signal bullish conditions
• Evaluate the **strength of an existing trend**
• Observe **dynamic support and resistance levels**

Although the default settings are commonly used, traders may adjust them to suit a specific asset or strategy. In most cases, only minimal adjustments are recommended to maintain consistency.

## Overbought vs oversold market conditions

Bollinger Bands® are particularly useful for identifying when prices may be stretched beyond typical levels.

### Overbought

When the price reaches the **upper band**, the asset is generally considered **overbought**. This suggests that the price may be relatively high in the short term. Some traders interpret this as a potential **sell signal**, expecting the price to move back toward the middle band.

![Bollinger Bands® Overbought](https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/331a126e-39e0-41b2-952d-a8ce72928e57)

### Oversold

When the price touches the **lower band**, the asset is often considered **oversold** and possibly undervalued. This situation may present a potential **buy signa**l, with the expectation that the price could return toward the average.

It is important to note that prices can remain overbought or oversold for extended periods. Contact with the bands does not automatically mean a reversal will occur. Many traders confirm signals using **additional indicators** or [**candlestick patterns**](https://www.easy-markets.com/au/learn-centre/understanding-analysis/identifying-candlestick-patterns-and-momentum/) before making decisions.

### Final Thoughts

Bollinger Bands® are widely regarded as a flexible and informative indicator. They help traders visualise volatility and identify possible entry and exit points. When combined with other analytical tools and used correctly, they can support improved timing and risk management.

As with any technical indicator, Bollinger Bands® do not guarantee outcomes. Traders are encouraged to confirm signals within a broader analytical framework and apply **appropriate risk management practices.**

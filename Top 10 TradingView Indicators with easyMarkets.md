# Introduction

[TradingView](https://www.easy-markets.com/eu/platforms/tradingview/) is widely used for charting and technical analysis. With a large number of indicators available, it can be difficult to know which ones are the most useful.

This guide focuses on key TradingView indicators that traders use to assess trends, confirm momentum, and identify potential entry and exit points. Whether you are new to trading or more experienced, these tools can support the development of your strategy.

## Understanding Trading Indicators on TradingView

A [TradingView indicator](https://www.tradingview.com/scripts/fibonacciretracement/) is a mathematical tool that converts price or volume data into visual signals displayed on a chart.

These signals help traders recognise trends, spot possible reversals, and identify important decision levels. TradingView includes a wide selection of indicators, ranging from moving averages to custom-built tools, designed for simplicity and strategy development.

## Top 10 TradingView Indicators

### 1\. Moving Average (MA)

The Moving Average is a widely used trend-following tool that smooths price data to show the overall direction of the market.

Traders often apply simple or exponential moving averages to identify support and resistance levels or to highlight potential reversal areas.

![Moving Average](https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/40af41df-f5c6-4c3c-b742-954a0a959b5b)

Find more information on the MA indicator on TradingView [here](https://www.tradingview.com/scripts/movingaverage/).

### 2\. Relative Strength Index (RSI)

Compared to Moving Averages, the RSI is used less for identifying overall trends and more for highlighting overbought and oversold conditions.

This indicator measures both the speed and size of price movements in an asset. It helps traders recognise when an asset may be overbought or oversold, which can indicate possible reversal points.

The RSI operates on a scale from 0 to 100:

- Values above 70 suggest overbought conditions
- Values below 30 suggest oversold conditions

![Relative Strength Index](https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/7f249a8e-a0a1-49db-9725-82eb186c3bd9)

Traders use RSI to help identify potential entry and exit points and to support trend strength analysis.

Learn more about the RSI indicator on TradingView [here](https://www.tradingview.com/scripts/relativestrengthindex/).

### 3\. Moving Average Convergence Divergence (MACD)

MACD is a commonly used **momentum indicator** that helps identify potential trend reversals.

It uses two moving averages to display both momentum and direction. When the MACD line crosses above the signal line, it may indicate a buy signal, while a downward crossover may suggest a sell signal.

The MACD histogram adds further insight into the strength of a trend.

![Moving Average Convergence Divergence (MACD)](https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/b9bdea8e-a8c1-4792-a26d-3a920d0ff359)

Find additional information on the MACD indicator on TradingView [here.](https://www.tradingview.com/scripts/macd/)

### 4\. Bollinger Bands

Bollinger Bands are used to measure volatility. The indicator consists of a moving average with upper and lower bands based on standard deviations.

Wider bands indicate increased volatility, while narrower bands suggest consolidation.

Traders use Bollinger Bands to help identify potential breakouts or reversals.

![Bollinger Bands](https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/0b1862f2-3ba1-4697-9d9e-4cfa6afed401)

Find additional information on the Bollinger Bands indicator on TradingView [here.](https://www.tradingview.com/scripts/bollingerbands/)

### 5\. Volume

Volume is used to confirm the strength behind price movements.

Increases in volume often occur during breakouts or major reversals, making it useful for assessing whether a trend is continuing or coming to an end.

![Volume](https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/76b5ba68-3821-4fe3-85e1-612e2aec02c6)

Learn more about the Volume indicator [here.](https://www.tradingview.com/scripts/volume/)

### 6\. Stochastic Oscillator

This momentum indicator compares an asset's closing price to its recent price range.

It helps identify overbought and oversold conditions. Crossovers between the %K and %D lines can signal potential entry or exit points.

![Stochastic Oscillator](https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/ca4aca2a-8e65-4003-9b05-b1749c8b1bdd)

Learn more about the Stochastic Oscillator on TradingView [here.](https://www.tradingview.com/scripts/stochastic/)

### 7\. Fibonacci Retracement

Fibonacci Retracement is a **support and resistance tool** that highlights potential trading insights into areas of possible support and resistance.

It is based on the Fibonacci sequence, where each number is the sum of the two preceding numbers. This tool helps identify key price levels. These levels indicate areas where the marker might reverse its current trend, pull back, or stabilise.

![Fibonacci Retracement](https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/ffc6948b-881c-42bf-94f9-c0340fe715f9)

This tool offers precise, measurable levels that help traders fine-tune:

- Stop-loss orders
- Define profit targets
- Identify when to enter the market

Find additional information on the Fibonacci Retracement indicator on TradingView [here.](https://www.tradingview.com/support/solutions/43000518158-fib-retracement/)

### 8\. Average True Range (ATR)

ATR measures market volatility rather than direction.

It shows how much an asset typically moves within a given time period, helping traders determine appropriate stop-loss levels.

![Average True Range (ATR)](https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/e93e7fe9-ef79-47b1-a3bf-ae98858e03da)

Learn more about the ATR indicator on TradingView [here.](https://www.tradingview.com/support/solutions/43000501823-average-true-range-atr/)

### 9\. Parabolic SAR

Parabolic SAR is a trend-following indicator that displays dots above or below price.

These dots indicate direction, and when they switch position, it may signal a reversal.

It is commonly used to help set trailing stop-loss levels.

![Parabolic SAR](https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/dc5e6c6b-6ae3-41b8-95d5-4133dc00545d)

Find additional information on the Parabolic SAR indicator on TradingView [here](https://www.tradingview.com/support/solutions/43000502597-parabolic-sar-sar/).

### 10\. Ichimoku Cloud

The Ichimoku Cloud is an all-in-one indicator that provides information on trend strength, momentum, and support/resistance levels.

Although visually complex, it offers a comprehensive view of market structure and is often used by more experienced traders interested in deep market structure analysis.

![Ichimoku Cloud](https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/a78a6eab-c046-4c5b-97d7-6ca08f76f645)

Traders analyse how the different Ichimoku lines interact to identify the best moments to start or end trades.

For example, traders using the Ichimoku Cloud may interpret price moving **above the Cloud** as a buy signal, while price moving **below the Cloud** may be seen as a signal to sell. If price is moving **within the Cloud**, this may suggest that the market is remaining flat.

The **colour of the Cloud** can also provide insight into market direction. A **green Cloud** — where **Leading Span A (Green Line)** is positioned above **Leading Span B (Red Line)** — may indicate an uptrend, while a **red Cloud** may point to a downtrend.

The Cloud's **26-day forward projection** can help traders anticipate possible support and resistance levels, which may offer an advantage when planning strategy.

Beyond helping identify potential entry and exit points, these signals can also support the placement of **stop-loss orders** to manage risk more effectively.

The different lines within the indicator can also offer insight into **overall market sentiment**.

By bringing together multiple elements of technical analysis into one framework, the Ichimoku Cloud can help traders make more informed trading decisions and better anticipate market movements.

Want to learn more about the Ichimoku Cloud? Read more on TradingView [here.](https://www.tradingview.com/scripts/ichimokuclouds/)

## A Word of Caution

While indicators can support decision-making, relying on a single tool or misinterpreting signals can lead to mistakes.

Examples include:

- Misreading Moving Average crossovers in unstable market conditions
- Relying on RSI without confirming the broader trend
- Using Fibonacci levels without additional confirmation
- Misinterpreting Ichimoku Cloud signals without understanding its components

It is important to confirm signals using multiple tools and consider the overall market context.
